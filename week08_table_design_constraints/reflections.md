# Week 8 Reflection — Tables, Constraints, and Aggregate Queries

This week changed how I think about SQL. Instead of starting with queries,
I had to start with **structure and rules**.

That shift made everything else more predictable.

---

## What I Learned

### Table Design Comes First

Designing tables with the right data types and constraints makes querying
easier later. When the table is well designed, queries feel simpler and safer.

---

### Constraints Are Not Optional

Constraints are not just “extra rules” — they are essential. They prevent
bad data from entering the system and reduce the need for complex checks in queries.

---

### Aggregates Depend on Good Data

Aggregate queries only make sense when the underlying data is reliable.
This week showed how design and analysis are tightly connected.

---

## Challenges

The biggest challenge was keeping track of multiple constraints and understanding
how they interact. Small syntax mistakes could cause errors, but working through
them improved my confidence.

Manually validating aggregate queries was slow, but it helped me trust my SQL much more.

---

## Key Points from This Week

- Good table design prevents problems later.
- Constraints enforce data integrity.
- `ALTER TABLE` should be used carefully and intentionally.
- Aggregate queries are depend on clean data.

---

## What I’ll Focus on Next

I want to get more comfortable designing tables for larger, more
realistic datasets and understanding how design choices affect performance.

This week felt like moving from “writing SQL” to **building databases I can rely on**.
